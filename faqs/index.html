Frequently Asked Questions on Acquihiring

Meta Description: Answers to the most searched questions on acquihiring — meaning, process, valuation, deal structures, ESOPs, and examples in India.

What is an acquihire?

An acquihire is when a company acquires a startup mainly for its team and culture, not its products, customers, or revenue.

What is the meaning of acquihire?

Acquihire means "acquisition for hiring" — the acquirer buys a startup to absorb its employees quickly.

How is acquihire different from acquisition?

An acquisition focuses on business assets and market share. An acquihire focuses on employees and know-how.

What is a typical acquihire price per engineer in 2024?

In India, acquihires often cost ₹20–40 lakh per engineer, depending on skills, scarcity, and location.

How is acquihire valuation calculated?

Valuation is based on cost-to-hire benchmarks, investor recovery, and strategic premiums for scarce talent.

What is an acquihire deal structure?

Common structures include business transfers, asset purchases, share purchases, or direct hiring with retention packages.

What is an acquihire term sheet?

A term sheet outlines employees included, consideration, ESOP treatment, founder roles, and investor settlements.

What is reverse acquihire?

Reverse acquihire is when a team joins a larger company while the startup entity continues independently.

What is the acquihire process?

The process includes scouting, term sheet signing, due diligence, definitive agreements, and integration.

Can you share acquihire examples?

Examples include Ola–TaxiForSure, Zomato’s logistics team acquihire, Flipkart–AI startup, and Razorpay–fintech startup.

What happens to ESOPs in an acquihire?

ESOPs may vest early, be cashed out, or be swapped into the acquirer’s stock option plan.

Do investors benefit in acquihiring?

Investors usually recover part of their capital but rarely make large profits from acquihires.
