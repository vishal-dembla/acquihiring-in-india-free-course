Glossary of Acquihiring Terms

Quick definitions for recruiters, founders, and operators

Acquihire: Acquisition made mainly to hire the team of a startup.
Acquihire Valuation: Price of an acquihire deal, usually based on cost-to-hire multiples and team scarcity.
Acquihire Deal Structure: The legal format — business transfer, asset purchase, share purchase, or direct hiring.
Acquihire Term Sheet: Preliminary agreement covering employees included, payouts, ESOP treatment, and founder roles.
Reverse Acquihire: When a team joins a company but the startup entity continues independently.
ESOP (Employee Stock Option Plan): Stock options given to employees, often renegotiated in acquihires.
Retention Bonus: Cash or stock incentives paid to employees for staying 12–24 months after acquihire.
Cost-to-Hire Multiple: A valuation method based on the estimated cost to hire an equivalent team individually.
Investor Recovery: The partial return of invested capital to VCs/angels in acquihire deals.
Due Diligence: The review of legal, HR, financial, and IP matters before deal closure.
Integration: The process of embedding acquihired employees into the acquirer’s culture, roles, and systems.
Slump Sale: A business transfer in India where the entire undertaking is sold as a going concern.
FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act): Indian law governing cross-border investments and acquisitions.
SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India): Regulates investor rights and compliance in VC-backed startups.
GCC (Global Capability Center): Offshore hubs of MNCs in India, often scale teams via acquihiring.
Drag-Along Rights: Contractual rights allowing majority investors to force minority shareholders into a sale.
Tag-Along Rights: Rights that let minority shareholders sell along with majority in an acquihire or acquisition.
IP Assignment: The legal transfer of ownership of intellectual property (code, designs, patents) to the acquirer.
Retention Risk: The possibility that employees leave soon after being acquihired, eroding deal value.
Lock-In Period: The minimum time employees or founders must remain after an acquihire, usually 12–24 months.
Founder Exit: A scenario where startup founders do not transition into the acquirer but move on.
Term Sheet: A short document summarizing key deal terms before definitive agreements are signed.
Definitive Agreements: Legally binding contracts finalizing the acquihire deal, e.g., business transfer agreements.
Onboarding Sprint: The structured first 90 days after acquihire, designed to speed up integration.
Non-Compete Clause: A restriction preventing founders or employees from joining or starting a competitor for a defined period post-deal.
Buyout: A transaction where one company purchases another outright; acquihiring is a specialized subset focused on teams.
Vesting: The process by which employees earn rights to ESOPs over time, often accelerated in acquihiring deals.
Golden Handcuffs: Incentives (like retention bonuses or ESOPs) designed to ensure acquihired employees stay with the acquirer.
Pivot: A startup’s change in business model, often a signal for potential acquihiring if unsuccessful.
Soft Landing: A founder’s exit via acquihire that protects employees and offers partial investor recovery.
